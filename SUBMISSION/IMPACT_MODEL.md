# S.O.M.I.S Impact Model: Quantifying the Alpha Gap

**Submission for Problem Statement #6: AI for the Indian Investor**

## 1. The Baseline: Manual "Ripple" Analysis
Tracing a second-order market impact manually is an intensive process reserved for institutional hedge fund analysts. For a retail investor, the process looks like this:
1. **Source Monitoring:** Refreshing ET Markets, CNBC, and Twitter (15 mins/day).
2. **First-Order Identification:** Reading an article and identifying the primary company (5 mins).
3. **Supply Chain Research:** Researching who the suppliers and customers are for that company (20 mins).
4. **Ticker Cross-Referencing:** Checking which of those secondary companies are listed on the NSE/BSE (10 mins).
5. **Reasoning & Validation:** Building a conviction case for the trade (15 mins).

**Total Manual Time per Opportunity: ~65 Minutes**

---

## 2. The S.O.M.I.S Advantage: Autonomous Swarm
S.O.M.I.S automates this entire pipeline using an agentic swarm and a local private brain.
1. **Parallel Harvesting:** Scraps ET, CNBC, and Reuters simultaneously (Seconds).
2. **Autonomous Reasoning:** Phi-3.5 traces the supply chain via the Knowledge Graph (60-90 Seconds).
3. **Alpha Alert Generation:** Immediate visual identification of the secondary target.

**Total S.O.M.I.S Time per Opportunity: ~1.5 Minutes**

## 3. Quantified Business Impact

### A. Efficiency & Time Saved

### B. Research Cost Reduction (Institutional Grade)
* **Metric:** Cost of hiring a Junior Equity Analyst in India vs. running S.O.M.I.S.

### C. Alpha Capture Potential (The Revenue Gap)
* **Concept:** First-order news (e.g., "Tata Motors profit up") is priced into the stock within minutes. 
* **The Opportunity:** Second-order ripples (e.g., "The steel supplier for Tata Motors will see a surge in demand next quarter") often take **hours or days** to be fully priced in by the market.
* **Impact:** S.O.M.I.S identifies these targets before they are "piled on" by the retail crowd, capturing early-entry alpha that is typically invisible to the average investor.
